Worth a visit

The six best resorts in Poland on the Baltic Sea

Sopot
The famous Sopot, picturesque and fashionable, offers not only a comfortable stay on the beaches, but also an extensive cultural and entertainment program. And you can also do almost any kind of sport here.
Ustka
The former fishing village of Ustka pleases visitors with cozy beaches with golden sand, gentle sea, magnificent sights. Every year thousands of tourists flock here to enjoy the ancient charm of the city and improve their shattered health.
Yastshembya Gura
Sharp cliffs, lush greenery and spacious beaches — all this is a delightful Yastzhembya Gura, located on the territory of the Nadmorsky Landscape Park. The name of the city means "hawk mountain" in translation. The key feature of the resort is that you have to go down to the beach from a cliff, the height of which is 30 meters.
Kolobzheg
You can't just take a vacation in Poland without visiting Kolobrzeg. It is considered the largest Polish health resort
Loeb
A truly unique resort — Leba - became famous thanks to the moving dunes, moving at a speed of 3-10 meters per year. The water approaches the city from the Baltic Sea and two lakes - Sarbsko and Lebsko. Slovinsky National Park, protected by UNESCO, is located nearby.
Svinoujscie
This resort is located on 44 islands, only 4 of them are inhabited. It is located surrounded by forest lands and is open all year round. Mineral waters, healing mud and favorable climatic conditions attrac t tourists here who want to relax with health benefits.

National dishes

Enjoy an unforgettable taste

Soups

At home and in restaurants, soups are cooked according to traditional village recipes. The basis of soups is, as a rule, meat (or less often vegetable) broth, where meat, sausages, vegetables, boiled eggs, pasta or cereals are added, so Polish soups are very different from mashed soups, more typical for southern Europe.
